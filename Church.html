Church

The Church aspect of Silliman University reflects its strong foundation in Christian faith and values. It emphasizes spiritual growth, moral development, and community fellowship through regular worship services, chapel activities, and outreach programs. Rooted in its Presbyterian heritage, the university integrates faith and learning, encouraging students to embody servant leadership and live out the principles of faith, hope, and love in their daily lives.

FOUNDERS WEEK DEVOTION -

DATE: August 19 2024 7:30 am- 9:00 am

ORGANIZER: College of Computer Studies

INVOLVEMENT: Sub-Organizer - 3 (Food Committee)

What? On August 19, 2024, from 7:30 AM to 9:00 AM, I participated in the Founders Week Devotion for the College of Computer Studies at Silliman University. This morning worship service marked the beginning of the university's Founders celebration. The event brought together students, faculty, and staff in a spirit of faith and gratitude. As part of the food committee, my role was to help prepare and organize the breakfast that followed the worship service. Our committee ensured that the food was ready and served smoothly, contributing to the warm and welcoming atmosphere.


So What? This activity taught me the value of collaboration and attention to detail. Initially, I expected it to be a simple service, but I was pleasantly surprised by the depth of connection and reflection it inspired. The worship was more than just a ceremony, it became a meaningful reminder of Silliman’s legacy and the values we uphold as a community. One of the most impactful moments for me was seeing the joy and gratitude on everyone’s faces as they shared a meal together. It was a humbling experience to know our efforts as the food committee added to their overall experience. The most satisfying part was the sense of accomplishment in creating an environment where everyone felt welcomed and appreciated. On the other hand, the most challenging aspect was ensuring everything was ready on time, but it taught me the importance of planning and teamwork. This experience also reinforced my values of service and compassion, as I realized how small contributions can make a significant difference in fostering a sense of community.


Now What? This experience has deepened my appreciation for the sense of belonging and faith that events like these bring. It taught me that even small roles, like helping with food preparation, can have a meaningful impact. I plan to continue volunteering for similar activities within the College of Computer Studies and beyond, as I now see how such efforts strengthen bonds within our community. If given the chance to participate again, I would focus more on coordinating with the team ahead of time to ensure even greater efficiency. This activity reminded me of the benefits of working together toward a common goal and the joy of seeing the community come together.


Because of this activity, I am... more aware of the importance of faith, service, and unity in fostering a strong and compassionate community.
